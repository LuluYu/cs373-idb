Chichen Itza Mexico

Chichen Itza was a large pre-Columbian city built by the Maya people of the Post Classic. The archaeological site is located in the municipality of Tinum, in the Mexican state of Yucatán. It was one of the largest Mayan cities and it was likely to have been one of the mythical great cities, or Tollans, referred to in later Mesoamerican literature. Nowadays, Chichen Itza is one of the most visited archaeological sites in Mexico and has an estimated 1.2 million tourists visiting the ruins every year.
